# Windhoek Comprehensive Research

## Top Attractions & Landmarks

1. **Christuskirche** - Iconic German Lutheran Church (1910), neo-Gothic/Art Nouveau, local sandstone
2. **Independence Memorial Museum** - Free entry, 9am-5pm, closed Sunday, built by North Korea, statue of Sam Nujoma
3. **Alte Feste** - Oldest building in Windhoek, former German Fort, National Museum (temporarily closed)
4. **Parliament Gardens (Tintenpalast)** - German colonial era building, statues of independence heroes
5. **Heroes' Acre** - Memorial honoring Namibian independence fighters

## Cultural Experiences

1. **Katutura Township Tour** - 200,000+ residents, authentic local life, Chameleon Safaris
2. **Kapana at Oshetu Market (Single Quarters)** - Famous street BBQ, grilled beef strips, vetkoek
3. **Free Walking Tours** - Local guides, history of Namibia, Chameleon Safaris
4. **Hair Braiding** - Fidel Castro Street, $100 NAD (~$7 USD)

## Dining & Restaurants

## Nightlife & Entertainment

2. **Hilton Casino** - Slots, roulette, poker

## Shopping

1. **Maerua Mall** - Major shopping center
2. **The Grove Mall of Namibia** - Modern mall
3. **Wernhil Park** - Central mall

## Nature & Wildlife (Near Windhoek)

1. **Daan Viljoen Nature Reserve** - 20 min from city, hiking, zebra, kudu, oryx, 200+ bird species
2. **Naankuse Wildlife Sanctuary** - 35 min drive, Brad Pitt/Angelina Jolie foundation, cheetah walks ($100), carnivore feeding ($50)
3. **Voigtland Guesthouse** - Giraffe feeding/lunch, 30km east, limited to 8 visitors/day
4. **Avis Dam Nature Reserve** - Walking trails, outside city center

## Hip Areas

1. **Am Weinberg** - Upscale area, coffee shops, galleries, Soulstice Day Spa, bakeries

## Practical Info

- **Taxis**: $24 NAD door-to-door, shared rides, apps: LEFA, TaxiConnect (no Uber)
- **Safety**: One of safest African capitals, use common sense
- **Days needed**: 1-2 days for main attractions
- **Altitude**: 1,700m
- **Population**: ~450,000
